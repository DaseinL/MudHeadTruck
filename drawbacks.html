The Drawbacks of Artificial Intelligence

Eliminating jobs in economic aspect

Although artificial intelligence has contributed to the economy as a whole, for some individuals and groups this has increased market competition. New production relations promoted by AI often lead to the elimination of old production relations and this is generally regarded by people as AI snatching their jobs.


An example in the communications industry is that the profession of a telephone transfer operator was replaced by a mainframe computer, and at the same time, the profession of a communications engineer came into being. As long as society is progressing, this process is inevitable because people are constantly adapting to the new economic environment. In the transformation process of applying artificial intelligence in the industry, the country needs to pay special attention to how to allow some people who cannot adapt to survive. In this process, the government should reduce the harm suffered by vulnerable groups, especially the unemployed. (Milind and Eric, 2018) This is related to the economic interests of many people. In addition, unemployment caused by AI is also one of the social problems leading to that some people are hostile to this technology. Thus, the influence of AI in society should be considered.


Infringing personal privacy in social aspect

The negative impact of artificial intelligence on society is mainly reflected at the individual level. At present, the most concerned issues of AI are information security. While enjoying the convenience brought by AI, due to its high computing and analysis capabilities, it is considered a threat to privacy.


People may inadvertently reveal some seemingly unimportant personal privacy when using some apps or browsing the web [9]. (Benjamin and etc., 2019) Once this fragmented information is analysed and integrated by AI, it will have some unfavourable consequences for individuals. For example, some insurance companies will determine the next year's premium by collecting and evaluating clients' search habits, financial health and medical records. This kind of unethical business behaviour is difficult to achieve manually due to its fragmentation before using AI technology. Therefore, as digital life is increasingly inevitable, people can only seek protection by changing personal information security habits and promoting privacy legislation.


Currently there are some established laws which have begun to protect people ’s information security such as the European Union’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) [10]. (Benjamin and etc., 2019) This shows that some governments have begun to pay attention to the harm of improper use of artificial intelligence. With the improvement of the legal system, the adverse effect of this problem may be largely reduced.
